Salat Training Toolkit

Sowing the Seeds of Salat in the Heart

Beginning a Loving Relationship with Allah

1. For Parents: Allah's Love in Your Child's Heart

Have you ever wondered what your child's greatest strength will be as they grow up? A prestigious degree, a good career, or just wealth? Islam teaches us that a person's greatest strength is their deep relationship with Almighty Allah—and the bridge to that relationship is 'Salat'.

Many of us present Salat to children only as a 'rule' or an 'obligatory task'. As a result, they perceive it as a burden. But imagine if Salat becomes a safe haven for your child, where they can tell their Creator about all the joys and sorrows of their life?

This toolkit is not just for teaching the rules of prayer; it is a spiritual journey to build love and reliance on Allah in your child's heart. Let us hold our children's hands and start walking towards Jannah together.

Pro-Tip for Parents: Keep your voice soft and affectionate while reading this guide. Let your child feel that learning prayer is a celebration of joy, not a difficult test.

2. For Kids: Your Private Conversation with Allah

Dear little one,
Did you know? The Owner of this vast universe, who created the sun, the moon, and all the beautiful flowers, loves you very much! And do you know what's even more exciting? He wants to talk to you!

Salat is a magical way to talk to Allah. When you stand on the prayer mat, you actually become Allah's special guest. You can tell Him anything you want, everything that's on your mind.

This little guide will teach you how to perform Wudu beautifully, how to stand before Allah, and how to tell Him all the wishes of your heart. Are you with me on this adventure? Let's get started!

Fun Fact for Kids: Did you know? When you say 'As-Salamu Alaykum', the angels also greet you back! You are not alone; many angels stand beside you during your prayer.

3. How to Use This Toolkit?

  • Don't Rush: This is not a syllabus, it's a habit.
  • Read Together: Sit with your child for at least 10 minutes every day with this guide.
  • Celebrate: Celebrate every small progress with praise and a smile.
