Module 3

The Power of Purity

Wudu: A way to brighten the body and mind

Lesson 3.1: The 5 Times of Salat (5 Special Moments of the Day)

1. Core Spiritual Concept

Allah has called us to meet Him 5 times throughout the day. Think of it like 5 "Refresh Buttons" for your day. These times are set according to the position of the sun.

2. Names & Times of Salat

  • Fajr: Before sunrise, when the night ends and the world begins to light up.
  • Dhuhr: At noon, when the sun moves slightly west from being directly overhead.
  • Asr: In the afternoon, when the sun's heat decreases and shadows grow long.
  • Maghrib: In the evening, when the sun sets and the sky turns reddish.
  • Isha: At night, when everything becomes completely dark.
Fun Fact for Kids: "Did you know? Each prayer time reminds us that Allah is always with us—in the morning, afternoon, evening, and at night."

Lesson 3.2: Purity is Half of Faith (Why do we perform Wudu?)

Wudu is not just washing your hands and face; it's a way to clean your heart too. The Prophet (PBUH) said—"Purity is half of faith." When we perform Wudu, our small mistakes are washed away with the water, and we become bright with Noor (light).

Lesson 3.3: Step-by-Step Wudu

  1. Bismillah & Washing Wrists: Before starting Wudu, say—Bismillah. Then wash both wrists 3 times.
  2. Rinsing Mouth & Nose: Take water into your mouth 3 times and clean your nose with water 3 times.
  3. Washing Face: Wash your entire face from the forehead to the chin 3 times.
  4. Washing Arms: First the right arm, then the left arm up to the elbows 3 times.
  5. Wiping the Head (Masah): Wipe your entire head once with wet hands.
  6. Washing Feet: First the right foot, then the left foot up to the ankles 3 times.

Lesson 3.4: Special Dua After Wudu

After finishing Wudu, if you look at the sky and recite this dua, the 8 gates of Jannah are opened for you!

أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلٰهَ إِلَّا اللهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّদًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ

Transliteration: Ash-hadu al-la- ila-ha illallahu wa ash-hadu anna Muhammadan 'abduhu wa rasuluh.

Meaning: "I bear witness that there is no god but Allah, and I bear witness that Muhammad (PBUH) is His servant and Messenger."

Pro-Tip for Parents: "Children love playing with water. Use this to your advantage during Wudu. After they finish the steps, give them a minute to play with the water—it will increase their interest in performing Wudu."
Fun Fact for Kids: "Did you know? On the Day of Judgment, the Prophet (PBUH) will recognize us by the bright glow of our Wudu parts! Our hands, face, and feet will shine like stars."
