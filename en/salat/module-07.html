Module 7

Essential Duas

Talking to Allah all day long

Tajwid Key:
  • j = Normal 'J' sound.
  • z = Soft 'Z' sound (as in: ذ).
  • zw = Heavy 'Z' sound (as in: ض).
  • q = Deep 'Q' sound from the throat (as in: ق).
  • - = Sign for elongation (long vowel).

Lesson 7.1: Post-Salat Dhikr (The 3 Magic Phrases)

After finishing Salat, instead of rushing away, we stay on the prayer mat for a few moments to remember Allah. This makes our hearts feel even more peaceful.

  • SubhanAllah: 33 times. (How Perfect is Allah!)
  • Alhamdulillah: 33 times. (All praise is for Allah!)
  • Allahu Akbar: 34 times. (Allah is the Greatest!)
Fun Fact for Kids: "Did you know? When you count these phrases on your fingers, on the Day of Judgment, your little fingers will testify for you and say—'O Allah, this child used me to remember You!' Then your fingers will shine like Noor (light)."

Lesson 7.2: Ayatul Kursi (The Strongest Verse)

This is a very special verse of the Quran. It reminds us that Allah never sleeps and He is always watching over us. If you read this before sleeping, Allah sends an angel to guard you all night!

Alla-hu la- ila-ha illa- huwal hayyul qayyu-m.

Meaning: "Allah, there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of [all] existence."

Lesson 7.3: Dua for Parents (The Sweetest Gift)

Our parents do so much for us. The best gift we can ever give them is to ask Allah to take care of them. This is a gift that never runs out!

Rabbir hamhuma- kama- rabbaya-nee saghee-ra-.

Meaning: "My Lord! Have mercy upon them (my parents) as they brought me up [when I was] small."

Pro-tip for Parents: "When your child recites this Dua, give them a warm hug and a kiss. Tell them—'Allah is so happy with me today because of your Dua.' This will inspire them to pray for you regularly."

Lesson 7.4: Sleeping and Waking Up Duas

When we sleep, we are in Allah's protection. When we wake up, Allah gives us a brand new day as a gift. So, the first thing we should do is say 'Thank You' to Allah.

Sleeping Dua: Allahumma bismika amu-tu wa ahya-.

Meaning: "O Allah, in Your name I die (sleep) and I live."


Waking Up Dua: Al-hamdu lilla-hilladhee ahya-na- ba‘da ma- ama-tana-.

Meaning: "All praise is for Allah who gave us life after He had given us death (sleep)."

Lesson 7.5: Morning Dua (For Knowledge & Blessings)

Start your morning by asking Allah for things that will help you grow and be a better person.

اللَّهُمَّ إِنِّي أَسْأَلُكَ عِلْمًا نَافِعًا، وَرِزْقًا طَيِّبًا، وَعَمَلًا مُتَقَبَّلًا

Transliteration: Allahumma innee as-aluka ‘ilman naafi‘an, wa rizqan tayyiban, wa ‘amalan mutaqabbalan.

Meaning: "O Allah, I ask You for beneficial knowledge, goodly provision and acceptable deeds."

Lesson 7.6: Leaving Home Dua (The Shield of Protection)

When you step out of your house, read this to have angels guard you until you return!

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ، تَوَكَّلْتُ عَلَى اللَّهِ، لَا حَوْلَ وَلَا قُوَّةَ إِلَّا بِاللَّهِ

Transliteration: Bismillaahi, tawakkaltu ‘alallaahi, laa hawla walaa quwwata illaa billaah.

Meaning: "In the name of Allah, I place my trust in Allah, and there is no power nor might except with Allah."

Lesson 7.7: After Wudu Dua (8 Gates of Jannah)

Did you know? If you recite this after a perfect Wudu, all eight gates of Paradise are opened for you!

أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا عَبْدُهُ وَرَسُولُهُ

Transliteration: Ash-hadu an laa ilaaha illallaahu wahdahu laa shareeka lahu wa ash-hadu anna Muhammadan ‘abduhu wa Rasooluhu.

Meaning: "I bear witness that there is no god but Allah alone, without any partner, and I bear witness that Muhammad is His servant and His Messenger."

Lesson 7.8: Sayyidul Istighfar (The Master Prayer for Forgiveness)

This is the most powerful way to say sorry to Allah. If you read this with sincere belief in the morning and pass away that day, you will be among the people of Paradise.

اللَّهُمَّ أَنْتَ رَبِّي لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ، خَلَقْتَنِي وَأَنَا عَبْدُكَ، وَأَنَا عَلَى عَهْدِكَ وَوَعْدِكَ مَا اسْتَطَعْتُ، أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا صَنَعْتُ، أَبُوءُ لَكَ بِنِعْمَتِكَ عَلَيَّ، وَأَبُوءُ لَكَ بِذَنْبِي فَاغْفِرْ لِي فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَغْفِرُ الذُّنُوبَ إِلَّا أَنْتَ

Transliteration: Allahumma anta Rabbee laa ilaaha illaa anta, khalaqtanee wa ana ‘abduka, wa ana ‘alaa ‘ahdika wa wa‘dika mas-tata‘tu. A‘oodhu bika min sharri maa sana‘tu. Aboo-u laka bini‘matika ‘alayya wa aboo-u laka bidhanbee faghfirlee fainnahu laa yaghfiru-dhunoo-ba illaa anta.
