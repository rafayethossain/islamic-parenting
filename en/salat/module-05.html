Module 5

Learning Salat (Part 2)

From Sujud to Salam: The moment of being closest to Allah

Tajwid Key:
  • j = Normal 'J' sound.
  • z = Soft 'Z' sound (as in: ذ).
  • zw = Heavy 'Z' sound (as in: ض).
  • q = Deep 'Q' sound from the throat (as in: ق).
  • - = Sign for elongation (long vowel).

Lesson 5.1: Sujud (Prostration): The Moment of Being Closest to Allah

1. Core Spiritual Concept

Sujud is the most special part of Salat. The Prophet (PBUH) said that when we perform Sujud, we are closest to Allah. It's like placing your head under Allah's Throne. You can silently tell Allah everything you want during this time.

سُبْحَانَ رَبِّيَ الْأَعْلَى

Transliteration: Sub-hana Rabbiyal A'la-. (3 times)

Meaning: "Glory be to my Lord, the Most High."

Fun Fact for Kids: "Did you know? When you perform Sujud, Shaitan becomes very sad and goes away crying! Because he didn't listen to Allah, but you, even though you are small, are listening to Allah."
Pro-Tip for Parents: "Tell your child that Sujud is the 'Secret Door to Allah'. Encourage them to silently tell their little wishes to Allah before rising from Sujud."

Lesson 5.2: Tashahhud (Attahiyyatu): The Conversation of Mi'raj

1. Core Spiritual Concept

Tashahhud is the conversation the Prophet (PBUH) had with Allah at the Arsh-al-Azim on the night of Mi'raj. When we recite it, we remember that blessed moment.

At-Tahiyya-tu Lilla-hi was-Salawa-tu wat-Tayyiba-tu. (All compliments, prayers, and pure things are for Allah.)

Fun Fact for Kids: "When we raise our index finger during Tashahhud, we testify that Allah is only One. It's like giving a 'thumbs up' to Allah—'Allah, I only love You!'"

Lesson 5.3: Durood Sharif & Dua Masurah (Love for the Prophet)

At the end of Salat, we pray for peace and blessings for our beloved Prophet Muhammad (PBUH). Then we ask Allah for forgiveness for our mistakes.

Dua Masurah: Allahumma inni zwalamtu nafsi zwulman kaseera-.

Meaning: "O Allah! I have wronged my soul greatly (made many mistakes)."

Lesson 5.4: Salam: Spreading the Message of Peace

1. Core Spiritual Concept

We finish Salat with Salam. By looking to the right and then to the left, we wish peace for the angels and people around us.

السَّلَامُ عَلَيْكُمْ وَرَحْمَةُ اللهِ

Transliteration: As-Sala-mu 'alaykum wa rahmatullah.

Fun Fact for Kids: "When you turn for Salam, do you know who you are looking at? The two angels (Kiraman Katibin) on your shoulders who are writing down your good deeds. You are greeting them—'Peace be upon you!'"
