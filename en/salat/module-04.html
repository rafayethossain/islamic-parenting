Module 4

Learning Salat (Part 1)

From Standing to Ruku: The first step in being humble

Tajwid Key:
  • j = Normal 'J' sound.
  • z = Soft 'Z' sound (as in: ذ).
  • zw = Heavy 'Z' sound (as in: ض).
  • q = Deep 'Q' sound from the throat (as in: ق).
  • - = Sign for elongation (long vowel).

Lesson 4.1: Facing the Qibla and Niyyah (Mental Preparation)

1. Core Spiritual Concept

Before starting Salat, we need to prepare our body and mind. We face the Kaaba (Qibla) because it is the direction of Allah's house. 'Niyyah' means deciding in your heart that "I am now going to talk to Allah."

Fun Fact for Kids: "Did you know? When you face the Qibla, millions of people around the world are standing just like you facing the Kaaba! It's as if we all form a giant circle with the Kaaba in the middle."
Pro-Tip for Parents: "Don't force your child to memorize long Arabic Niyyah sentences. Explain that Allah knows what's in our hearts. Just ask them—'Who are you standing before?' That awareness is the best Niyyah."

Lesson 4.2: Takbir-e-Tahrimah and Thana

1. Core Spiritual Concept

When we raise our hands to our earlobes, we are leaving the world behind us. And 'Thana' is praising Allah before starting our conversation with Him.

اللهُ أَكْبَرُ

Transliteration: Alla-hu Akbar.

Meaning: "Allah is the Greatest."

سُبْحَانَكَ اللَّهُمَّ وَبِحَمْدِكَ وَتَبَارَكَ اسْمُكَ وَتَعَالَى جَدُّكَ وَلَا إِلَهَ غَيْرُكَ

Transliteration: Sub-hanaka Allahumma wa bi-hamdika wa taba-rakas-muka wa ta'a-la- jadduka wa la- ilaha ghayruka.

Meaning: "O Allah! I declare Your purity and praise You. Your name is blessed, Your majesty is supreme, and there is no god but You."

Fun Fact for Kids: "When we raise our hands for Takbir, it's as if we are saying to the world—'Bye bye world! Now I'm only going to listen to Allah!'"

Lesson 4.3: Surah Fatiha: A Conversation with Allah

1. Core Spiritual Concept

No Salat is complete without Surah Fatiha. It is a real conversation with Allah. When you recite each verse, Allah praises you before the angels and answers you!

1. Al-Hamdu Lilla-hi Rabbil 'A-lameen. (All praise is for Allah, the Lord of all the worlds.)

2. Ar-Rahma-nir Raheem. (The Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.)

3. Ihdinas-Sira-tal Mustaqeem. (Guide us to the straight and easy path.)

Pro-Tip for Parents: "Pause briefly while reciting Surah Fatiha. Tell your child that when they say 'Alhamdulillah', Allah says—'My servant has praised Me'. This interaction will keep the child engaged during prayer."

Lesson 4.4: Ruku and Qawmah: Being Humble before Allah

1. Core Spiritual Concept

Ruku means bowing your head before Allah. We only bow before Allah. Standing up straight from Ruku is called 'Qawmah'.

Ruku: Sub-hana Rabbiyal 'Azeem. (3 times)

Qawmah: Sami'allahu liman hamidah. → Rabbana- lakal hamd.

Fun Fact for Kids: "When performing Ruku, we keep our backs perfectly straight. It's like a superhero stretch that makes our body strong too!"
