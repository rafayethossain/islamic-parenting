Module 8

Parent Guide & Q&A

Encouraging Your Child with Kindness

Ramadan is not just a month for children; it is the beginning of a long-lasting spiritual experience. As a parent, your role is to make this journey joyful, easy, and inspiring. In this module, we will discuss how to keep children cheerful while they are hungry and how to build love for fasting in their hearts without being harsh.

Section 8.1: Make it Easy & Give Glad Tidings

1. Concept Summary

Islam instructs us to make our religion easy. When training children for fasting, our main goal should be to encourage them, not to scare them.

2. The Sacred Connection

يَسِّرُوا وَلاَ تُعَسِّرُوا، وَبَشِّرُوا وَلاَ تُنَفِّرُوا

Pronunciation: Yassiru wa la tu'assiru, wa bashshiru wa la tunaffiru.

Meaning: "Make things easy and do not make them difficult; give glad tidings and do not make people turn away."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (6125), Sahih Muslim (1734)

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): If a child shows an irritable mood while fasting, be patient. Remember, their body is feeling the lack of glucose. Instead of scolding, hug them calmly or encourage them to sleep for a while.

Section 8.2: Handling Hunger & Thirst

It is natural for children to feel physical discomfort when they fast. Through some strategic changes, we can reduce this hardship for them.

  • Nutritious Suhoor: Give children foods at Suhoor that provide slow-release energy (like oats, bananas, milk, nuts).
  • Sufficient Water: Encourage them to drink small amounts of water during the time from Iftar to Suhoor.
  • Distractions: Play indoor games, tell stories, or share interesting Quranic tales to help them forget about hunger.
  • The Magic of Praise: When they are enduring hunger, congratulate them and praise their patience.

Section 8.3: Encouragement vs. Enforcement

4 Golden Rules for Parents:

  1. Be a Role Model: Show yourself fasting cheerfully.
  2. Avoid Competition: Don't compare with other children.
  3. Small Milestones: Set small goals instead of the whole month.
  4. Rewards System: Give small gifts for their achievements.
🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? When you intend to do a good deed but cannot do it for some reason, Allah still gives you a full reward! Allah is so generous, isn't He?

Q&A for Parents

Q: From what age should I start encouraging my child to fast?
A: It is Sunnah to gradually get children accustomed to fasting from childhood (7-10 years). You can encourage your child to fast for short intervals based on their physical capacity.
Q: Are the 'Practice Fasts' or half-day fasts allowed?
A: For training, ‘Practice Fasts’ (staying without food until noon) are an excellent pedagogical tool. It gives children the mental strength to keep full fasts when they grow up.
Q: If a child eats something by mistake, should I tell them to continue the fast?
A: Definitely! According to the Hadith, eating by mistake is a gift from Allah. Don't scold them; rather, cheerfully tell them to continue the fast.
