Module 5

Manners & Sabr (The Golden Shield)

Did I Break My Fast?

Fasting is like following the rules of a beautiful game. Sometimes we make mistakes, and other times we don't understand what breaks the fast. In this module, we will learn what actions break our fast and how Allah forgives us if we eat or drink by mistake.

Section 5.1: Is Forgetfulness a Gift from Allah?

1. Concept Summary

If someone eats or drinks by forgetting that they are fasting, their fast is not broken. This is a special gift from Allah to His servant.

2. The Sacred Connection

مَنْ نَسِيَ وَهُوَ صَائِمٌ فَأَكَلَ أَوْ شَرِبَ فَلْيُتِمَّ صَوْمَهُ

Pronunciation: Man nasiya wa huwa sa'imun fa-akala aw shariba fal-yutimma sawmahu, fa-innama at'amahullahu wa saqahu.

Meaning: "Whoever forgets he is fasting and eats or drinks, let him complete his fast, for it is Allah who has fed him and given him drink."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (1933), Sahih Muslim (1155)

3. Heart-Centered Deep Dive

Analogy: ‘The Surprise Break’
Imagine you are fasting and you get very thirsty in the afternoon. You completely forget you are fasting and drink a glass of water. After drinking, you suddenly remember, "Oh no! I am fasting!" Is your fast broken? Not at all! The Prophet (pbuh) said that Allah loved you so much that He fed you. So, after drinking the water, stay calm and continue your fast!

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): If a child eats by mistake, don't scold them or tell them their fast is broken. Explain that it is Allah's mercy and encourage them to continue with patience. This strengthens their love and trust in Allah.

Section 5.2: What Actually Breaks the Fast?

Intentionally eating, drinking, or violating the rules of fasting breaks the fast. We must stay away from these actions.

Analogy: ‘The Red Light’

  • Intentional Eating or Drinking: If you know you are fasting and still purposely put food or water in your mouth.
  • Intentional Vomiting: If you purposely try to vomit (vomiting due to illness does not break the fast).
  • Intention to Break the Fast: If you decide in your mind that "I will not fast anymore" and start eating.
🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? Brushing your teeth (without paste or very carefully) or using a Miswak is allowed while fasting! It is a favorite act of the Prophet (pbuh).

Section 5.3: Green Lights: Common Myths

There are many things we think break the fast, but they actually don't. We can call these ‘Green Lights’:

  • Swallowing Saliva: It is natural to swallow saliva to keep the mouth clean; it doesn't break the fast.
  • Taking a Shower: Taking a shower to cool down the body doesn't break the fast.
  • Applying Oil or Lotion: Applying oil or cream to the body or hair doesn't break the fast.
  • Involuntary Vomiting: If you suddenly feel sick and vomit involuntarily, the fast is not broken.
💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): Children often have misconceptions that swallowing saliva or applying oil breaks the fast. Clear these myths for them. Make it clear that fasting is about controlling the will.

Section 5.4: What to do if a fast breaks?

The Action Plan:

  1. Tell the Truth: If you eat something, don't hide it from your parents.
  2. Repent (Tawbah): Ask Allah for forgiveness in your heart.
  3. Try Again: Don't give up if a fast breaks. Try to be Allah's friend again the next day.
🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? The taste of food in Jannah is millions of times better than food on Earth! The food you are giving up for Allah today, He will give you much more colorful and delicious food in Jannah.
