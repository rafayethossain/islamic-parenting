Q&A for Parents

Fasting for children is not just physical; it is a mental and spiritual training. As a parent, you may have many questions along this journey.

Q1: From what age should I start encouraging my child to fast?
A: Fasting becomes obligatory after puberty. However, it is Sunnah to gradually get children accustomed from childhood (7-10 years). The Sahaba (Companions) used to have their children fast and would distract them with toys if they felt hungry.
Q2: What if the child gets very hungry or thirsty long before Iftar?
A: If you see your child is exhausted or struggling significantly, do not force them. Explain that they have shown great patience and that Allah is pleased with their effort. Let them break the fast and don't make them feel guilty.
Q3: How to manage school and exam pressure during Ramadan?
A: If a child gets too tired during exams, let them fast only on holidays. Teach them that studying is also a form of worship. Provide sufficient protein and fiber at Suhoor to keep their brain active.
Q4: Are 'half-day fasts' allowed?
A: For training purposes, 'half-day fasts' (staying without food until noon) are an excellent pedagogical tool. It builds the mental strength to keep full fasts later.
Q5: My child feels sad when others eat in front of them. What should I say?
A: Remind them of 'Taqwa' or their secret friendship with Allah. Tell them, "Only those whom Allah loves can sacrifice their favorite food for Him. You are Allah's special friend."
Q6: If a child eats something by mistake, should I tell them to continue the fast?
A: Absolutely! According to the Hadith, eating by mistake is a gift from Allah. Cheerfully say, "Allah loved you so much He fed you today, now continue your fast."
Q7: Is there any risk to the child's health?
A: For healthy children, fasting usually causes no harm if proper Suhoor and Iftar are ensured. However, if the child is ill, consult a doctor. Ensure plenty of fluids at night.
Q8: Is it okay to take children to Taraweeh prayers?
A: If your child can stay relatively quiet, definitely take them. It introduces them to the spiritual atmosphere. However, if they are tired, let them rest.

Infographic Guide

1. The Fasting Clock

1. Suhoor: Eat the blessed Suhoor meal.

تَسَحَّرُوا فَإِنَّ فِي السَّحُورِ بَرَكَةً

Tasahharu fa-inna fis-suhuri barakah.

2. Imsak & Fajr: Make intention and pray.

3. Morning Deeds: Recite the Quran.

4. Patience & Play: Be patient and stay cheerful.

الصبر ضياء

As-Sabru Diya (Patience is light).

5. Iftar Prep: Set the table and make Dua.

6. Iftar: Joyful Iftar! Say the Dua and break the fast.

ذَهَبَ الظَّمَأُ وَابْتَلَّتِ الْعُرُوقُ...

Dhahabaz-zama'u wabtallatil-'uruq...

2. 5 Manners of Iftar

  1. Hasten Iftar: Start as soon as the Adhan is called.
  2. Make Dua: Allah accepts prayers just before Iftar.
  3. Dates & Water: "Fa-innahu Barakatun" (For it is a blessing).
  4. Say Bismillah: Before the first bite.
  5. No Wasting: "Eat little, gain more reward."
