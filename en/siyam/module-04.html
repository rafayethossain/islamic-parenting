Module 4

The Joy of Iftar (The Victory Table)

Allah's Gift After a Day of Patience

Iftar is a special reward from Allah after waiting all day. When the sun sets and the sound of the Adhan fills the air, our hearts fill with joy. In this module, we will learn the Dua for Iftar, the Sunnah of breaking the fast with dates, and some beautiful manners to follow during Iftar.

Section 4.1: Iftar: A Moment of Double Joy

1. Concept Summary

Iftar is not just about eating; it is a special celebration from Allah. There are two special moments of joy for the person who fasts, and one of them is Iftar.

2. The Sacred Connection

لِلصَّائِمِ فَرْحَتَانِ: فَرْحَةٌ عِنْدَ فِطْرِهِ، وَفَرْحَةٌ عِنْدَ لِقَاءِ رَبِّهِ

Pronunciation: Lis-sa'imi farhatani: farhatun 'inda fitrihi, wa farhatun 'inda liqa'i Rabbihi.

Meaning: "The fasting person has two joys: one when he breaks his fast, and the other when he meets his Lord."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (1904), Sahih Muslim (1151)

3. Heart-Centered Deep Dive

Analogy: ‘The Champion's Feast’
Imagine you participated in a big competition and worked hard all day. How do you feel when you finally get the trophy? Iftar is like getting that trophy. All day you endured hunger and thirst for Allah, and at the time of Iftar, Allah rewards you with His blessings. Only those who fast can truly understand this joy!

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): Make the time of Iftar festive for children. Let them help arrange their favorite foods on the table. The few minutes just before Iftar are a time for Dua to be accepted, so encourage them to make small Duas.
🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? Every day at Iftar, Allah Almighty frees many people from the Fire and writes their names on the list of the people of Jannah. Don't you want your name to be on that list?

Section 4.2: The Iftar Dua

1. Concept Summary

When breaking the fast, we recite a special Dua taught by the Prophet (pbuh) that takes away the day's fatigue and confirms our reward.

2. The Sacred Connection

ذَهَبَ الظَّمَأُ وَابْتَلَّتِ الْعُرُوقُ، وَثَبَتَ الأَجْرُ إِنْ شَاءَ اللَّهُ

Pronunciation: Dhahaba-zama'u wab-tallatil 'uruqu, wa thabatal-ajru in-sha-Allah.

Meaning: "The thirst has gone, the veins are moistened, and the reward is confirmed, if Allah wills."

Source: Sunan Abu Dawood (2357) — Hasan

Analogy: ‘Freshness After Rain’
Just as the leaves of a withered sapling become fresh when rain falls on it, our bodies become fresh after having a sip of water after fasting all day. By reciting this Dua, we acknowledge that Allah has quenched our thirst and turned our hardship into reward.

Section 4.3: Dates & Water: The Sunnah Way

إِذَا أَفْطَرَ أَحَدُكُمْ فَلْيُفْطِرْ عَلَى تَمْرٍ فَإِنَّهُ بَرَكَةٌ

Pronunciation: Idha aftara ahadukum fal-yuftir 'ala tamrin fa-innahu barakah.

Meaning: "When one of you breaks his fast, let him break it with dates, for they are a blessing."

Source: Sunan At-Tirmidhi (695) — Sahih

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): Natural Sugar Tip: Dates are a great source of natural sugar, which quickly raises blood sugar levels and removes body fatigue. Give the child at least 1-2 dates before eating fried foods.
🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? The leaves of the date palm never fall! It is like a Mu'min or a believer, whose good deeds always continue.

Section 4.4: Golden Rules of Iftar

Iftar is a Royal Feast given by Allah. Therefore, we should sit quietly and follow these manners:

  1. Break Fast Early: Break the fast as soon as the Adhan starts, without delay.
  2. Make Dua: Make Dua for yourself and everyone else just before Iftar.
  3. Say Bismillah: Say Bismillah before you start eating.
  4. Don't Waste: Only take as much on your plate as you can eat.
💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): Ask children to help with Iftar preparations (setting the table, pouring water). This makes them feel like a part of this worship and helps them forget their hunger or irritation.
