Module 2

Why We Fast (The Secret Meeting)

Our Secret Friendship with Allah

In this module, we will learn the true purpose of Siyam (fasting) and why Allah has asked us to stay away from food and drink. We will discover our special secret friendship with Allah and how fasting makes us patient and kind individuals.

Section 2.1: Our Secret Friendship with Allah

1. Concept Summary

Fasting is the only act of worship that no one else can see. It is a special secret agreement between us and Allah, which increases our honesty and love for Him.

2. The Sacred Connection

قَالَ اللهُ تَعَالَى: كُلُّ عَمَلِ ابْنِ آدَمَ لَهُ إِلَّا الصِّيَامَ، فَإِنَّهُ لِي وَأَنَا أَجْزِي بِهِ

Pronunciation: Qalal-lahu Ta'ala: Kullu 'amali-bn-Adama lahu illas-siyama, fa-innahu lee wa ana ajzee bihi.

Meaning: Allah Almighty said, "Every deed of the son of Adam is for himself, except fasting. It is for Me, and I will give the reward for it."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (1904), Sahih Muslim (1151)

3. Heart-Centered Deep Dive

Analogy: ‘The Invisible Gift’
Imagine your friend gave you a delicious chocolate. You are alone in your room, and no one is watching you. You could eat it if you wanted, but you didn't because your friend told you not to. This act of keeping your word even when you are alone is called ‘Taqwa’ or love for Allah. Fasting is an act of worship that only you and Allah know about. You might be standing with a glass of water in your hand, no one is looking, but you don't drink—because you know Allah is watching you. This is your special secret friendship with Allah!

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): When talking about ‘Taqwa’ with children, emphasize ‘love’ and ‘honesty’ rather than ‘fear’. Explain that when they follow Allah’s commands in private, they become Allah’s VIP friends. This builds their self-esteem and moral strength.
🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? There is a special door in Jannah called ‘Ar-Rayyan’. Only those who fast will enter through this door. Once the last person who fasted enters, the door will be closed. Do you want to enter through that special gate?

Section 2.2: Our Invisible Shield

1. Concept Summary

Fasting is not just about tolerating hunger; it is a powerful shield that protects us from bad deeds and anger.

2. The Sacred Connection

الصِّيَامُ جُنَّةٌ

Pronunciation: As-Siyamu Junnatun.

Meaning: "Fasting is a shield (that protects us from evil deeds)."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (1894)

3. Heart-Centered Deep Dive

Analogy: ‘Superhero Bodyguard & Digital Shield’
Do you know about superheroes? Just as they have shields to protect them from enemy attacks, fasting is an invisible shield for our hearts. This shield doesn't just protect us from lying or fighting; it also works as our ‘digital shield.’ For example—not getting angry when losing a game, or looking away if you see something bad on the internet. When someone tries to make you angry or a bad thought comes to your mind, use your shield and say, "Inni Sa'im" or "I am fasting!"

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): If a child becomes irritable due to fasting, don't scold them. Remind them that fasting is a ‘shield’ and a means of ‘peace’. Ask them, "Where is your shield? We shouldn't fight!" This encourages them to control their anger in a playful way.

Section 2.3: The Mirror of Hunger

1. Concept Summary

Fasting teaches us what the pain of hunger feels like. Through this, we can feel for those who don't have enough food every day.

2. The Sacred Connection

مَنْ فَطَّرَ صَائِمًا كَانَ لَهُ مِثْلُ أَجْرِهِ

Pronunciation: Man fattara sa'iman kana lahu mithlu ajrihi.

Meaning: "Whoever provides Iftar for a fasting person will have a reward like theirs."

Source: Sunan At-Tirmidhi (807) — Sahih

🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? Angels pray for the person fasting all day long until they break their fast. When you fast, millions of angels are telling Allah, "O Allah! Forgive this little child and give them many rewards!"

Section 2.4: Planting the Seed of Patience

إِنَّمَا يُوَفَّى الصَّابِرُونَ أَجْرَهُمْ بِغَيْرِ حِسَابٍ

Pronunciation: Innama yuwaffas-sabiruna ajrahum bi-ghairi hisab.

Meaning: "Indeed, the patient will be given their reward without account."

Source: Surah Az-Zumar, Verse 10

Analogy: ‘A Tiny Sapling’
Patience is like a small sapling. A tree doesn't grow huge in one day. It has to endure sun and rain and be watered regularly. Only then does it give sweet fruit one day. Our patience is like that. Before Iftar, when you are very thirsty, if you hold on with a little patience, the joy you feel after Iftar is greater than any other joy in the world. This habit will help you succeed in many big tasks in the future.
