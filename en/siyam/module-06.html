Module 6

Mini-Mu'min Deeds (Ramadan Good Deeds)

Special Rewards of Ramadan

Ramadan is a giant fair for earning rewards! In this month, the reward for every good deed is multiplied many times. In this module, we will learn how we can make our Ramadan more colorful and blessed through Taraweeh prayer, Quran recitation, and charity (Sadaqah).

Section 6.1: Taraweeh: A Wonderful Night with the Quran

1. Concept Summary

Taraweeh is a special prayer during Ramadan nights where we stand for a long time and listen to the words of Allah or the Quran. It calms our minds and brings us closer to Allah.

2. The Sacred Connection

مَنْ قَامَ رَمَضَانَ إِيمَانًا وَاحْتِسَابًا غُفِرَ لَهُ مَا تَقَدَّمَ مِنْ ذَنْبِهِ

Pronunciation: Man qama Ramadana imanan wahtisaban ghufira lahu ma taqaddama min dhanbihi.

Meaning: "Whoever stands in prayer during the nights of Ramadan with faith and seeking reward, his previous sins will be forgiven."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (37), Sahih Muslim (759)

3. Heart-Centered Deep Dive

Analogy: ‘Walking in the Gardens of Jannah’
Standing in Taraweeh prayer and listening to the Quran is like walking in the gardens of Jannah. When the Imam recites the Quran in a beautiful voice, angels of mercy descend from the sky to surround us. Even if you cannot pray the full 20 or 8 Rak'ahs, try to pray at least a few with your parents. It will build you as a brave and patient friend of Allah.

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): Don't pressure children to get used to Taraweeh. Give them their favorite prayer mat or cap/hijab. Let them pray as long as they can spontaneously. Let them drink some water or rest in between prayers to make it enjoyable for them.

Section 6.2: Quran: Allah's Letter to Us

شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ الَّذِي أُنزِلَ فِيهِ الْقُرْآنُ هُدًى لِّلنَّاسِ

Pronunciation: Shahru Ramadanal-ladhi unzila fihil-Qur'anu hudal-linnas.

Meaning: "The month of Ramadan is that in which was revealed the Quran, a guidance for the people."

Source: Surah Al-Baqarah, Verse 185

Analogy: ‘The Magic Map’
The Quran is a magic map for our lives. How we should live, how we should speak, and how we should go to Jannah—everything is written in this map. In Ramadan, we open this map more often. For every letter you read, Allah gives you 10 rewards (Hasanat)!

🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? When you read the Quran, your surroundings fill with a fragrance that we might not smell, but the angels do! Angels see the house that reads the Quran as brightly as we see stars from Earth.

Section 6.3: Sadaqah: Spreading Smiles

كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ (সা.) أَجْوَدَ النَّاسِ فِي رَمَضَانَ

Pronunciation: Kana Rasulullahi (pbuh) ajwadan-nasi, wa kana ajwada ma yakunu fi Ramadana.

Meaning: "The Messenger of Allah (pbuh) was the most generous of people, and he was even more generous in Ramadan."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (6), Sahih Muslim (2308)

Analogy: ‘Planting Reward Trees’
Giving charity is like planting an invisible sapling. When you help a poor person, a huge fruit tree is created for you in Jannah. In Ramadan, for every dollar you give, Allah gives you 70 times the reward! Sadaqah is not just about giving money, but also your good behavior and helping attitude.

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): Make a ‘Sadaqah Box’. Every day, put some money from your savings there. At the end of Ramadan, use this money to help an orphan or a poor child.

Section 6.4: 10 Mini-Deeds for Kids

  1. Be the Salam King: Be the first to say Salam to everyone at home and outside.
  2. Iftar Helper: Help arrange glasses or dates on the Iftar table.
  3. Arrange the Prayer Mat: Fold your and your parents' prayer mats after prayer.
  4. Quiet Worship: Stay quiet when adults are praying.
  5. Bird Feeder: Put some water and seeds for birds on the roof or windowsill.
  6. Memorize a Small Surah: Learn at least one new Surah during the month.
  7. Water the Plants: Take care of Allah's creation.
  8. Thank Your Parents: Say ‘Jazakallahu Khayran’ to those who prepare Suhoor and Iftar for you.
  9. Sweet Smile: Speak to everyone with a cheerful smile.
  10. Dua Diary: Make a special Dua to Allah every day before Iftar.
