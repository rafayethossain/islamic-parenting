Module 3

Suhoor & Intention (Power-Up: The Fasting Day)

The Blessed Start of the Fasting Day

The fasting day begins when the world is very quiet and the stars are twinkling in the sky. In this module, we will learn about the blessing of Suhoor, how to make the intention (Niyyah) in our hearts, and how to spend the day with joy and worship.

Section 3.1: Suhoor: The Blessed Meal

1. Concept Summary

Suhoor is the special meal before the fast that gives us energy throughout the day. It is not just food; it is a Sunnah of the Prophet (pbuh) and contains many blessings.

2. The Sacred Connection

تَسَحَّرُوا فَإِنَّ فِي السَّحُورِ بَرَكَةً

Pronunciation: Tasahharu fa-inna fis-suhoori barakah.

Meaning: "Eat Suhoor, for indeed there is blessing in Suhoor."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (1923), Sahih Muslim (1095)

3. Heart-Centered Deep Dive

Analogy: ‘Super-Fuel’
Imagine you are going on a long journey in a rocket. Before the rocket takes off, it needs to be filled with plenty of fuel. Suhoor is that ‘Super-Fuel’ for our bodies. While everyone is asleep, Allah organizes a special feast for us. Waking up half-asleep to eat Suhoor with your parents is a great adventure! This meal keeps our bodies fresh all day and prepares our minds for the worship of Allah.

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): Try to have your child's favorite food at Suhoor to encourage them. Don't force them to eat too much; let them enjoy the spiritual atmosphere of the time.
Sleepy Suhoor Tip: If the child is very sleepy, instead of heavy food, give them at least a glass of water and 3 dates. This fulfills the Sunnah and provides necessary energy.

Section 3.2: Niyyah: The Promise of the Heart

1. Concept Summary

Niyyah means deciding in your heart that "I am fasting today solely for the sake of Allah." It is not a difficult mantra, but a beautiful promise with Allah.

2. The Sacred Connection

إِنَّمَا الأَعْمَالُ بِالنِّيَّاتِ

Pronunciation: Innamal a'malu bin-niyyat.

Meaning: "Indeed, the results of every deed depend on the intention (Niyyah) behind it."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (1)

Analogy: ‘The Heart’s Compass’
When a ship sails in the sea, it has a compass that tells it where to go. Niyyah is that compass for our minds. Why are we staying without food all day? Because we love Allah. Even if you don't recite a long prayer, if your heart knows why you are fasting, your Niyyah is done! This is a secret promise between you and Allah.

Section 3.3: Fasting Window & ‘Practice Fasts’

The time from Fajr to Maghrib is called the fasting period. For young children, we start practicing patience through ‘Practice Fasts’ or half-day fasts.

Analogy: ‘The Marathon of Patience’
The fasting day is like a marathon race. From Imsak (when we stop eating) until Iftar (when we break the fast), we strengthen our patience. If you are very young, you can keep a ‘Practice Fast’ until noon. This prepares you for keeping full fasts when you grow up.

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): In many cultures, there's a concept of 'Half-day fasts' for kids. While not a Shariah requirement, use it as a pedagogical tool to get them accustomed. Praise their effort and let them know Allah is pleased with their small steps.

Section 3.4: Imsak: The Story of the White Thread

وَكُلُوا وَاشْرَبُوا حَتَّى يَتَبَيَّنَ لَكُمُ الْخَيْطُ الْأَبْيَضُ مِنَ الْخَيْطِ الْأَسْوَدِ مِنَ الْفَجْرِ

Pronunciation: Wa kulu washrabu hatta yatabayyana lakumul-khaytul-abyadu minal-khaytil-aswadi minal-fajr.

Meaning: "And eat and drink until the white thread of dawn becomes distinct to you from the black thread of night."

Source: Surah Al-Baqarah, Verse 187

Analogy: ‘The Game of White and Black Threads’
In ancient times, when people didn't use clocks, they could look at the sky and understand when to stop eating. Allah said that when the light of dawn appears like a white thread and separates from the dark night like a black thread, the time for Suhoor is over. It’s like a magical moment!

🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? The time of Suhoor is a special time for Dua to be accepted. Allah is ready to listen to whatever you ask Him at this time. So, after Suhoor, ask Allah for at least one thing!
