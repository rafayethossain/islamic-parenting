Module 7

Laylatul Qadr & Eid (The Best Night & The Big Feast)

The Ultimate Rewards of Ramadan

Towards the end of Ramadan, we look for a very precious night, and then comes our long-awaited joyful Eid. In this module, we will learn why ‘Laylatul Qadr’ is better than a thousand months and how we can celebrate a joyful Eid by following the Sunnah.

Section 7.1: Laylatul Qadr: The Night Better Than 1000 Months

1. Concept Summary

Laylatul Qadr is the most powerful and blessed night of the year. Worshipping on this one night is better than worshipping for 1000 months (about 83 years)!

2. The Sacred Connection

لَيْلَةُ الْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍ

Pronunciation: Laylatul-Qadri khayrum-min alfi shahr.

Meaning: "The Night of Qadr (Decree) is better than a thousand months."

Source: Surah Al-Qadr, Verse 3

3. Heart-Centered Deep Dive

Analogy: ‘The Time Machine Night’
Imagine you have a magical night. If you do just 1 good deed that night, Allah will give you rewards as if you did that deed continuously for 83 years! This is called Laylatul Qadr. Allah has hidden this night in the odd nights of the last 10 days of Ramadan. We should look for this night like detectives and worship more. On this night, Jibril (as) and millions of angels descend to earth with peace and mercy.

💡 Pro-Tip (For Parents): Loosen children's sleep routines slightly during the last 10 nights. Engage in Dua and Dhikr with them. Explain that it is like a special "Treasure Hunt" where the prize is Allah's love and Jannah.

Section 7.2: The Special Dua of Qadr

اللَّهُمَّ إِنَّكَ عَفُوٌّ تُحِبُّ الْعَفْوَ فَاعْفُ عَنِّي

Pronunciation: Alla-humma innaka 'afuwwun tuhibbul-'afwa fa'fu 'anni.

Meaning: "O Allah, indeed You are Forgiving and You love to forgive, so forgive me."

Source: Sunan At-Tirmidhi (3513) — Sahih

Analogy: ‘The Heart Cleaner’
Just as we clean our hands and face with soap, this Dua is the soap that cleans the dirt of our minds. We might have made many mistakes throughout the year, but if we recite this Dua, Allah wipes away all our mistakes and makes our hearts completely clean like new.

🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? When you recite this Dua, angels stand beside you and say ‘Amin’ (O Allah, accept)!

Section 7.3: Joyful Eid: Our Reward (Eid-ul-Fitr)

لِكُلِّ قَوْمٍ عِيدٌ وَهَذَا عِيدُنَا

Pronunciation: Li-kulli qawmin 'eedun wa hadha 'eeduna.

Meaning: "Every nation has a festival, and this (Eid) is our festival."

Source: Sahih Bukhari (952), Sahih Muslim (892)

Analogy: ‘The Champion's Grand Party’
Ramadan is like a training camp where you showed a lot of patience. And Eid is a huge celebration or grand party after successfully finishing that training! On this day, we wear beautiful clothes, eat delicious food, and have fun with friends. Allah wants us to be happy on this day and share the joy with each other.

🌟 Fun Fact (For Kids): Did you know? On the morning of Eid, angels stand at the street corners and say, "O believers! Go to your Merciful Lord, He will give you a huge reward!"

Section 7.4: Sunnahs of Eid Day

Our dear Prophet (pbuh) had some beautiful habits to make Eid day more blessed:

  • Take a shower and wear clean clothes.
  • Apply perfume (Attar).
  • Eat something sweet before the Eid prayer.
  • Recite the Takbir softly while going to the Eidgah.
  • Change the route while going and returning.

اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ لاَ إِلَهَ إِلاَّ اللَّهُ وَاللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ اللَّهُ أَكْبَرُ وَلِلَّهِ الْحَمْدُ

Pronunciation: Allahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, La ilaha illallahu wallahu Akbar, Allahu Akbar, wa lillahil-hamd.

Meaning: Allah is the Greatest, Allah is the Greatest. There is no deity except Allah. Allah is the Greatest, Allah is the Greatest, and all praise is for Allah.
